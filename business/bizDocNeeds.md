1. The "What Is This Business" Document (Company Profile)Before onboarding someone, you need a high-level, highly polished overview of your business. This can be a 2–4 page PDF or a slide deck.Key Sections to Include:Executive Summary: A 1-page "cover letter" outlining your business goals, expertise, and what you do.Mission & Vision: Your core purpose, company culture, and where you are headed.Core Offerings: Clear, simple descriptions of your products, services, or proprietary technology.Market Position & Traction: Who your target customers are, current revenue milestones, or notable growth data.The Team: Brief bios of the founders or executive team to build credibility.👉 Where to find templates: You can browse Canva's Company Profile Templates or use professional layouts via Microsoft Word Business Templates to format this cleanly.2. The Strategic Pitch Document (Partnership Proposal)If the partner is "potential" and not yet signed, you need a document that explains why this partnership makes sense for both parties.Key Sections to Include:The Opportunity: The market gap or problem you want to solve together.Value Proposition: What your company brings to the table, and what they bring.Proposed Joint Venture/Model: How you will work together (e.g., co-marketing, referral, revenue share, tech integration).Financials / Expectations: High-level sales targets, revenue expectations, or commission structures.👉 Where to find templates: Platform sites like Pitch's Partnership Proposal Template provide free, customizable slide frameworks built specifically for this transition.3. The Onboarding Welcome Package (Signed Partners)Once a business partner agrees to join forces, you transition to an active onboarding framework to get them up to speed quickly. This is usually delivered as a Welcome Kit or digital checklist.[Phase 1: Legal & Systems] ──> [Phase 2: Training & Assets] ──> [Phase 3: Joint Action Plan]
What the Welcome Package Should Contain:Welcome Letter: A brief, warm greeting setting a positive tone for the relationship.The Partner Onboarding Checklist: A clear, step-by-step roadmap of what they need to complete.Administrative & Legal Access: Links to execute contracts, NDA documents, and instructions to access internal portals or communication tools (like Slack or Teams).Resource Hub / Sales Playbook: Access to product training materials, brand guidelines, marketing assets, and a list of internal points of contact.Mutual Action Plan (MAP): A shared 30-60-90 day goal timeline outlining KPIs and early milestones.Summary Checklist for Partner IntegrationTo structure your documentation build, use this general lifecycle sequence:Step 1: Introduction — Send a short Business Introduction Letter alongside your Company Profile.Step 2: Proposition — Present the Partnership Proposal Presentation to formalize details.Step 3: Security — Execute formal agreements, NDAs, and compliance checks.Step 4: Onboarding — Issue the Welcome Kit with system access and your training checklist.Step 5: Alignment — Host a formal kickoff meeting to define mutual SMART goals.To help narrow down the right format, what is the exact nature of this partnership (e.g., are they a distributor, investor, co-founder, or software partner)?
